Zoroak

Previous Next


As the final project in AP Physics, we created Zoroak, a remote soil analyzer. In a group of five, we used Raspberry Pi and Python to create an autonomous soil arm that extracts soil samples and analyzes the light spectra, temperature, and soil density. OpenCV was used to calibrate position and depth. Motion is adjusted in real time.

European Girls’ Mathematical Olympiad 2018

Previous Next


In 2018, I earned a bronze medal at the European Girls’ Mathematical Olympiad (EGMO). Selected as one of four Canadian students to represent Canada in EGMO, I pursued three months of intensive training before travelling to Florence, Italy to take the two day-long contest. While the contest was intellectually stimulating, the social aspect of EGMO was what made it so memorable. We spent a week exploring Italy and meeting girls from 50 other countries who were equally passionate in mathematics and the STEM fields. Reinvigorated, I sought out opportunities in my own community to promote girls and women in STEM as we continue to break barriers in traditionally male-dominated fields, such as mentoring for Girls Learning Code and maintaining my own Robotics blog to make robotics more accessible to students.

Arduino

In high school, I dabbled in working with and robotics. After learning to code in C and familiarizing myself with the Arduino, I began creating my own projects and prototypes.

Room Monitor
I prototyped a room monitor, which gathers surrounding measurements (eg. Light intensity, temperature, height), and displays it on an oLED. The prject underwent several version controls, where from each update, new sensors and features were added.

Brick Breaker
I attempted to recreate the iconic game Brick Breaker using an oLED display as a monitor, push button, and potentiometer as a controller. I used it as an outline for one of my Arduino workshops that I organized in high school.

CubeSat Detection

In the summer of 2017, I worked as a lab assistant for Dr Jian-Feng Shi in his research on optimizing the detection of cube satellites using convoluted neural networks. I used OpenCV to create real-time computer vision and gain further knowledge in machine vision. For his research, I helped extract ground data of more than 300 images to train neural networks. I was later acknowledged in his paper, "CubeSat Simulation and Detection using Monocular Camera Images and Convolutional Neural Networks" DOI: 10.2514/6.2018-1604

Book Blogging

It started with teardrops on a paperback copy of Heartless by Marissa Meyer and a need to rant. Tapping vigorously away on the keyboard, I stumbled through a rabbit hole into the eccentric and lively community of book bloggers. With a collective love for literary and figurative worlds, we gushed over fairy tale retellings and declared our love for favorite book tropes. From reaching out to authors to host exclusive interviews to promoting an up and coming book, there is never a dull moment in the book community. No longer is reading a solitary activity; book blogging has become my favorite pastime amidst the chaos of everyday life.
